Project #2 Experimental Camera Part2

Introduction

I created 6 different classes of gestures to generate 6 different music melodies. The main purpose of my work is to combine human gestures with music. It will recognize the speed, orientation, front and back of different gestures, and play different music and video effects. In the future, Teachable Machine will further recognize more complex human gestures, such human body movements, and combine these movements to play various musical instrument melodies. With this tool, people will not have to concern about what music and video effects to choose, they can randomly create their own simple favorite movements and just leave the complex creative process to the tool. Therefore, the purpose of this project is to allow people to be musicians or dancers anytime, anywhere.

Working Progress

I have shown several demos to my peer, and we have found two main issues on my tool: 1) The accuracy of tool gestures recognition. 2) The lack of richness on canvas display. In order to solve First issues, I improved the recognition of my gestures in machine learning tool. A slow-motion camera will be used, which can capture 240 photos per second, to capture the details of my every gesture to make the system clearly recognize the changes of gestures. In addition, I have to change the wrist joints. The direction of rotation and the motion of waving, and distinguish between two hands and one hand to increase the accuracy of tool gestures recognition. For the lack of canvas display, I also improved the visual effects, and made a greater distinction between the appearance of the graphics on the canvas, such as different colors, sizes of bars, and numbers of appearances. As a result, various visuals can make the tool more interesting and easier to interact.

Reflection

Even though there is a good demonstration of the functionality of the teachable machine tool, but the it is very sensitive to the surrounding user's environment, because this tool is only captured by recording static pictures of gesture changes with the white background, so although I did a lot of testing as well as the improvement. However, the only issue is that if the background behind the user is not white since the system only recognizes white walls, some gesture changes will not be detected very well, resulting in a bit of confusion in the music variations. Therefore, in order to prevent tool effects by the user's background, I will improve the recognition and positioning of the tool's algorithm for gestures, by blurring the user's background to enhance the depth of field effect, and reduce the impact of the background to increase the accuracy of the tool.
