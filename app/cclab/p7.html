Project #7 Bitmap Game

Link to Code Sketch

Proposal

My bitmap game derives its inspiration from Lewis Caroll’s famous novel Alice in Wonderland. The game character is Alice herself when she finds herself shrinking in size after drinking a mysterious potion in a room. The design of the game space is thus based on that particular scene. In addition, the game borrows from the novel, and the contextual era it created in, to include many ornamentation of Victorian themes, such as flowers, furnitures etc I sketched. That scene where Alice is panicking and searching a cure anxiously upon seeing her body getting smaller somehow really struck me as a kid, even though I don’t remember all the details of the story. That’s the reason why I’ve used this scene as the creative background of my game. I believe it’s really fun to see an ip from fairy tales that everybody is familiar with set in a bitmap game.

Game Description

Alice falls down the rabbit hole while chasing a hare with a pocket watch. Unexpectedly, she finds herself in a beautiful room and, out of curiosity, swallows a bottle of magical potion. Now Alice is shrinking! The opening frame of the game has floral decoration on all four sides and brief instructions on how to play the game, a quick “how to play” section. During the game, to prevent Alice from totally disappearing as she gets smaller, the player must use the four arrow keys to guide her to travel up, down, left, and right in order to obtain the potion, which will emerge at random spots during the game. The more potions Alice collects, the higher the score of each game will be, which is kept at the top of the screen. If Alice gets too small and vanishes, the game is over, which triggers the textual indicator “game over” and prompts the player to play the game again by pressing space.

Reflection

For the design of this game, I drew a lot of inspiration from the well-known story of Alice in Wonderland and my own feelings of enchantment and curiosity upon first hearing about the magical and fantastical elements of the world as a child. I incorporated a number of Victorian ornaments as well as whimsical motifs that occurred in the story, in the design of the imagery that decorates the frames of the interface. In the opening frame, we see classical Victorian floral arrangements that is visually pleasing to the eye. In the gameplay, we see such elements as chairs, clocks, mushroom and poker soldiers etc. This visual design, along with the representation of the game character Alice, conveys a sense of elegance and more importantly playful wonderment. The sound design complements the visual design in creating a light-hearted playful mood. The score music in the opening frame is lively and melodious. During the game, a similar sound plays every time Alice obtains a new potion. Overall, the game is a light-hearted and whimsical one that employs a traditional reward and punishment system. The rapidly shrinking image of Alice creates a sense of urgency and motivates the player to quickly find new potions so as to avoid defeat. The disappearance of your game character is a direct and literal visual translation of failure in this game. On the other hand, the increase in size, as well as the increase in the score kept conspicuously at the top of the interface offer rewards that prompt the player to keep playing in hopes of getting a better score. This game is simple in its mechanism but very fun to play. It takes the player into a fantastical world, one that Alice finds herself in, where quirky and amazing things can happen.
