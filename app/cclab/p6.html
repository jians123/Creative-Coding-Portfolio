Project #6 Data Portrait

Link to Code Sketch

Design Description

In this project, I plan to keep track of my drinking habits. In my daily life, I am quite fond of drinking. Part of the issue is that I workout a lot and am frequently thirsty. Another reason is that many individuals believe that drinking more water can increase my body's metabolism and improve my health. As a result, I started to keep track of my drinking habits.

Instruction

I began recording my data on Last Wednesday and finished on Last Saturday. Every day between 10 a.m. and 8 p.m., I recorded how many glasses of water I drank every hour on my phone. As a result, the numbered list I have documented are as follows: Wednesday =,0,0,2,0,4,1,2,3,1 [3]; Thursday =,1,0,1,3,2,2,1,2,0 [2]; Friday =,2,0,0,1,2,3,5,1,0 [3]; 1,3,0,4,2,1,2,0,1 Saturday = [0]. Each value in the array reflects the number of glasses of water I consumed during that hour of the experiment. In the p5js sketch, I created four rows of dancing balls. The balls indicate the overall frequency with which I drank water during the course of the four-day trial. I computed the average quantity of water I drank each day and came up with [1.6,1.4,1.7,1.4]. I changed the frequency of dancing to demonstrate the difference in daily water use.

Design Process

Because I needed to capture and visualize my data, I do not want to just enter the data into p5js, which would result in a dull product. To go in with the subject of "self-portrait," I used Photoshop to create a cartoon-style image of myself drinking. This may help to a better display of my "drinking water" habit. I took four rows of colorful balls, arranged them in an array, and positioned them in the location of "my" stomach. Their dance has the same frequency as water flowing. I wanted to mimic and accentuate the movement of drinking water by doing this.

Reflection

I discovered that by producing a data portrait, I can really accomplish something quite fascinating, which is to use ordinary and unnoticed data in my everyday life to create an engaging and appealing product. I never considered using everyday items to make products. My biggest takeaway from this endeavor is that it has encouraged me to pay attention to more fascinating and everyday topics. Aside from that, data visualization may be used to spark new ideas. I'll look for additional opportunities to do this. When it comes to my losses, one notable one is that I did not devote enough time to the project. This will be improved in future developments.
