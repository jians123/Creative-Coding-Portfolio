Project #2 Face Generator

Design Process

I built a size of 400*400px canvas with illustrator and made a draft of the face on it. The face was made up of redness around the eyes, and the nose shaped like stars. Then I sketched a curve to represent the mouth. The expression was innovative but simple. I did the largest and smallest faces separately, with the largest almost taking up the entire canvas. Then, in the most comprehensive face, I isolated the eyes, the star, the nose, and the mouth, exported them in PNG format, and imported them into p5.js. I altered some of the coordinates of the coordinate system to place the components in different spots. Then I changed the data in the random to modify the vibration amplitude of the face, which makes the entire product funny. However, what bothers me is that when I exported each individual component of the face into the P5JS, I had to adjust the data range of the instruction one by one. I configured various backdrop color to change automatically as the mouse moved randomly on the screen, which corresponds to the face's size shifting. In addition, I included some stars in my piece that will appear and burst on the screen when people press the right mouse button. This, I feel, is what distinguishes my work. >

Design Sketch

Reflection

I discovered some great and helpful techniques while working on this project dubbed "Face Generator." For example, by varying the data range and random data, I was able to change the form, size, and movement of the image. And the burst of stars was created by pressing the mouse. I hope that individuals who engage with my work have fun and pleasure when they see this humorous expression and click the right mouse button to relieve their pressure.
