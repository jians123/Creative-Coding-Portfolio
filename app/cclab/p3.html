Project #3 Experimental Clock

Design Description

I came up with the concept of making a tree-shaped clock. The tree changes over time. In the spring, the tree's blossoms open up. Summer is a great time to see the leaves grow. In the late fall, the tree bears fruit. The tree then goes dormant for the winter, with no fruits or leaves to show for its efforts. As a result, the seasons of the trees served as a visual representation of the 24 hours in a day. The spring tree represents the beginning of the day, or 0 o'clock. During the summer, it is common to wake up at six a.m. Autumn starts at 12 o'clock and ends at 18 o'clock. The tree's growth, from the time its blossoms bloom in the spring until the time all of its leaves fall off in the winter, may then disclose the day's time.

Design Process

Fry eggs and the developing fetus in the mother's womb are the other two concepts I didn't end up adopting. I didn't use it for the first since I believed it was overused to utilize fried eggs to demonstrate the passage of time. I wanted to design a clock that could tell the time throughout the day. For the second, I didn't utilize it since it was difficult to determine which day of the fetus' development should be referenced. In addition, I discovered that using the seasons was a precise and straightforward method to display the passing of time during the day. There are four distinct seasons to the day.

Reflection

Since the if-else statement has a defined structure that permits instructions to be applied, this project taught me how to utilize it effectively. It's possible to transition from one picture to the next by clicking the mouse after the X and Y values are defined within an acceptable range. I split my 600x600px canvas into four equal halves. After that, I applied a transparent transition effect between the two photographs by reducing the change of season photos to 0.5 seconds. To my surprise, this effect only lasted for one round. If I tried to click the mouse to alternate between any two quarters after that initial round, the effect would disappear. The transition didn't loop until I used the bchanging instruction, which causes a transition to be created every time the mouse is clicked.
