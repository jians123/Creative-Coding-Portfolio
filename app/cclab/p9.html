Project #9 Consentful Interface

Link to Code Sketch

Design Progress

In the camera I designed, the interface is run through several steps. After entering into the web page, users can click on the right arrow in the top left corner and later an automatic pop-up box will ask for consent to turn on the camera. After receiving the users’ agreement, the web interface will be shown in Figure 1.0. At this page, if users move the cursor to the corresponding box marked red, they will be guided to hit the numbers 1-4 on the keyboard to run the camera and switch filters.

Description

I designed the page to protect user privacy based on two considerations. The first point is, while many camera applications on the market only send a one-time privacy permission prompt, I choose to design a double authentication system, using a special box trigger and keyboard toggle to ensure that the camera will revert to the consentful interface once the users’ mouse is removed from the box, thus protecting the users’ privacy every time. Another point is considered in terms of typography, I design different font size, color and styles serving as background reminders. For instance, the most eye-catching part is a line of words encouraging users to authorize camera privacy, rather than hiding it in a large paragraph with small text. By marking red and enlarging the font size, the users’ awareness of privacy will be subconsciously enhanced.

Reflection

In response to F.R.I.E.S, I mainly focused on both reversible and informed sides. Clear and suggestive explanations will be given to inform the user community about the information accessing to the camera. In addition, users are reminded of their rights to de-authorize the privacy permission to the camera at any time. The double verification approach may be relatively multi-step to use, but as a designer, I intended to convey my sincere and respectful attitude to protect user privacy. In designing the unconventional camera, I planned to balance the aesthetic diversity of the camera filters with the protection of user privacy. With the concept of the masses should be allowed to release from the societal aesthetic homogeneity and appreciate their imperfect selves, after redesigned the distorted mirror filter and created a new filter as the fourth option, I further transformed my idea into the consentful interface, conveying that all imperfections of users will be safely protected. In this way users are expected to feel reassured to use my camera and fully engage in their imaginative self-creations.
