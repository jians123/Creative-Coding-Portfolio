Project #4 Exquisite Corpse

Link to Code Sketch

Design Process

I started by drawing a nun in Illustrator. I retained the nun's head, and the rest of her body was based on designs supplied by my teammates(Amanda,Aisha,Shayla). I merged these various body parts to create a one-of-a-kind "monster." I adjusted the rising speed of the balloons behind the nun to a user-defined value so that I may raise them with a mouse click. Also,as you press the mouse, the nun's mouth will continue to open and close as she is reciting a religious hymn.

My Old Background Story

The nun was dressed in a black robe and a white turban, which seemed calm and elegant. A gray cross pendant lay quietly on the white piece of her chest. In Catholicism, the cross on the chest is a clothing accessory for the clergy of the monastery. This shows that her identity is unusual. However, her face was pale and her lips were blood red, giving a great visual impact. This will make people confused whether it is red lips or bloody mouth. Her eyes drooped and looked innocent, like a cute child. But the combination of the whole expression looks a little weird. Her calm face and big horrified eyes give people a sense of suppressed and unknown fear. She seemed to be staring at the person looking at her with pleading and fearful eyes. The combination of these elements makes her strange and complicated. This has to make people worry that her behavior and appearance will be very different.

My New Background Story

As a whole, it can be seen that this is still a nun. Compared with the actual image of a nun, this nun uses the geometric concept of a natural image. For example, her head is round, her turban is square, and her limbs are rectangular. Compared to the photo capturing reality, the image of this nun is obviously more abstract and cartoonish. Her body is the morph of a dice, and her feet are the morph of a mobile phone holder. It is well known that the image of a nun is a black robe with a white mind. More colors on their bodies may be their red lips. With the combination and proportion of these three colors, it is interesting to abstract their bodies as dice. Finally, the white and red balloons behind the nun deepen the cartoonish Ness of the nun's image, because the balloons represent innocence.

Shayla's Background Story

About Mr. Apple: I feel as though I tend to find myself surrounded by screens. My laptop sits directly in front of me while my phone rests beside it and my iPad plays music through my Bluetooth speaker. There are so many screens that I feel like sometimes people turn into them. Mr. Apple is a prime example, a screeny. He has three eyes from staring too intently at his technology (he had to add a third because two simply weren’t enough). His hair style is always in flux because it’s so versatile, all he has to do is think up a new one on the internet and wear it for a day. His shirts work the same way, as any image can become the newest fashion statement. Unless it involves buttons. Buttons are outdated. Side note, if the Wifi connection is really bad, he won’t be able to hear you very well. He gets that from all the Zoom calls. Lastly, we can’t forget his shoes. We all know how modern technology likes to walk all over USB-A's. Mr. Apple is a trend setter.

Amanda's Background Story

My backstory: The dominox is a machine with a human concept. The numbers on the dominoes represent the number of features on what a human face would have, organs placed as a human would have, and the neck as an empty domino piece to represent that the machine has no voice therefore it can only express in actions. The philosophy behind Dominox is simple as it sounds; dominoes are built to collapse only to be built & collapse again in different forms. But because it is a machine with a human concept, the dominox is paired with a domino power bank to give it a machine driven power to bring itself to life every time it collapses.

Reflection

I developed an intriguing addition to the "Stitched corpse" as a result of my work with my teammates. (I take Amanda's upper body, Shayla's lower body, and Aisha's balloon as the prop.) In line with the concept, I picked an unsettling but saintly music that lends the piece a holy air. I altered the parameters of the balloon, the nun's lips, and the crucifix behind her with a bespoke formula. This work has allowed me to investigate elements that I had never considered before.
