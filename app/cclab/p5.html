Project #5 Optical Illusion

Link to Code Sketch

Design Description

I come from a traditional Chinese background, and I would like to choose lotus flower to present optical illusion. Lotus is linked with many of the pristine qualities that one would like to possess. Thus, over China's long history, many renowned poets have used lotus to combine it with other beautiful words to create a magnificent poetry that the Chinese have read for thousands of years. It has now become a part of Chinese culture.

Design Strategies

I picked a photograph of a real lotus blossom as my draft in order to produce a realistic and vibrant visual illusion. After that, I edited it in Photoshop. For example, I put some additional petals to the lotus to depict the process of the flower from closing to blossoming. I created some straight lines in the photo and subsequently covered it with a graphic made up of several black lines to clearly depict each stage of lotus development. The audience may experience the illusion of the lotus flower opening or shutting in the express as the black lines move back and forth.

Reflection

I didn't utilize any complex coding in this project. I just considered changing the track of the movement of the black lines picture after importing the photo of lotus with some lines and the picture of black lines into P5JS. Two variables were used: t and n. T indicates the change's starting value, which is 0. N is the amount that is added to T each time. I set n to 0.5 since I used 0.5 as a unit of measurement comparable to resolution ratio, therefore it should be divisible by 600px. Because the black line travels horizontally, the x value in its coordinate axis should be changed to t. As a result, I wrote an if statement that causes the black lines to travel to the right when it starts playing, but when they reach the canvas edge, they begin to move to the left. In addition, I modified two functions: when the left mouse button is long pressed, the movement stops; when the mouse button is released, the movement resumes. Overall, because computer languages are so versatile, I believe logic is a critical component of generating animation effects using P5JS. It's the ideal option if you can accomplish your goals with few lines of code.
