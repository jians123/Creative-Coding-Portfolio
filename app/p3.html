Project #3 Handmade Website

Introduction

My handmade website was inspired by the poem "The Road Not Taken." This poem initially appeared in my basic school Chinese textbook. I was a naive youngster at the time, and I had no idea what the title of this poem meant. Despite the fact that it was just used as some beautiful sentences on the title page of a chapter, I was blown away by its appeal. I became increasingly drawn to this poetry as I grew older and realized its tremendous significance and implication. At the age of 38, the author Robert Frost left his comfortable and secure teaching job to devote his time to creating poetry. "Write poetry, if you're poor, you're poor," he added. His life is depicted in The Road Not Taken. Choosing one route necessitates the exclusion of another. Diverse decisions result in different lifestyles. What if the road ahead is full with thorns and bumps for the beloved and the ideal hidden in the heart? You can only live your style if you follow your inner need.

Working Progress

According to the rich philosophy, I built two roads based on the content in the poem, each signifying a fork in the path of a long life. Users can select the next interface to access based on their preferences. Following your selection, the interface will provide two distinct forms of road instructions. I created two diametrically opposed interactive scenarios to reflect the various sights along the trip on separate highways. The first option is an open avenue covered in golden leaves, which provides people with a relaxed and comfortable tranquil delight; the second option is a lush and quiet route lighted by firefly, which entices people to explore deeper surroundings. Despite their differing features, these two roads are gorgeous and flat, just asking for me to stomp. However, regardless of which option you choose, whether the landscape along the road is magnificent or light, users will finally arrive at the same joyful conclusion surrounded by trees and flowers. This also represents the poem's deeper meaning: in real life, persons at the complete intersection will undoubtedly feel the swing and wandering of decision, but as long as they make their own choice, they will have no regrets.

Reflection

By creating this interactive website, I've gained a stronger and more self-aware grasp of the link between choice and life. What I focus on in the process of creating the tableau of the two roads is how to portray the commonality and distinctiveness of the two roads via the comparison of interface design. I hope to accomplish the impact of allowing people to eventually end with an immersive tangled feeling. We can have a better knowledge of the connotation of poetry as a result of this encounter. The notion of achieving the same beautiful end through varied ways, as well as the superiority of experience, is reflected in reaching the same beautiful conclusion.
